Scottish Liberal Democrats Jamie Stone

Jamie Stone is the MP for Caithness Sutherland and Easter Ross. He's first came to Westminster in 2017 after serving for more than 10 years in the Scottish Parliament.

After the most recent leadership election, Jamie was asked to continue as Defence Spokesperson for the Lib Dems, as well as to take on the Digital, Culture, Media, and Sport brief. His main campaigns have included Diversity in the Armed Forces, Online Harms, Support for the Live Events Industries, and the Gaps in Support APPG. As Chair of this APPG, Jamie has been working relentlessly trying to secure financial support for people who have fallen through the gaps of Government coronavirus support.

Scottish Labour John Erskine

Born in Inverness and raised in Ross-Shire, John attended Maryburgh Primary school and Dingwall Academy before going on to study for a B.A.(Hons) in Philosophy and Politics and a Post-Graduate Diploma in Strategic Public Relations from the University of Stirling.

John stood as Highland Labour’s youngest candidate at the 2012 local government elections, narrowly missing out on election to the Dingwall and Seaforth ward, before then standing for Scottish Labour at the 2015 General Election in Caithness, Sutherland and Easter Ross, 2016 Scottish Parliament elections for the Highlands and Islands Regional List, and again in 2019 at the U.K. General Election in his home constituency of Ross, Skye and Lochaber. After University, John worked for the Highlands and Islands Scottish Labour MSPs Rhoda Grant and David Stewart in the Inverness regional office between 2012 and 2015, before going on to work in the Scottish Parliament as an advisor to Iain Gray MSP and then Kezia Dugdale MSP, during her time as Scottish Labour Party leader.

In 2019 John left his job with the Law Society of Scotland to stand as a candidate against SNP Westminster Leader Ian Blackford and is now seeking to be elected for Scottish Labour in the Highlands and Islands following the retirement of veteran MSP, David Stewart and is currently campaigning to be #1 on the Highlands and Islands List for Scottish Labour. He currently works for Iain Gray MSP, David Stewart MSP and the Coop as a Member Pioneer for the Dingwall area supporting local good causes.

Scottish National Party Maree Todd

After 20 years of experience working in health care, mostly as a mental health pharmacist in Inverness, Maree Todd was elected to the Scottish Parliament in 2016 to serve as a list SNP MSP for the Highlands & Islands region.

In 2017, Maree was appointed as the Minister for Children & Young People in the Scottish Government. Since becoming a minister, Maree has been involved in delivering on some of the Scottish Government’s most transformational policies, from the baby box to the expansion of early learning and childcare.

Scottish Conservatives Edward Mountain

Edward Mountain is a Member of the Scottish Parliament for Highlands and Islands Region (Scottish Conservative).

He is one of the seven regional MSPs for the Highlands and Islands Region having been elected on 6 May 2016. Edward is also the Convener of the Rural Economy and Connectivity Committee of the Scottish Parliament.

Edward’s area of interests includes health care provision in the Highlands, improving infrastructure, and renewing the Highland school estate. Edward also supports a strong local economy and is passionate about agriculture and fisheries.

Before entering politics Edward enjoyed a long career as a soldier. He joined the army in 1980 and his military service took him to Cyprus, Uganda, Germany, Canada, United Kingdom, Spain and Egypt. Edward left the army in 1992 but continued to serve as a reservist for a further six years. After leaving the army, Edward went on to qualify as a chartered surveyor in 1997, becoming a senior partner for a firm based in Inverness in 2006.

In 2007, Edward returned home to farm and runs a herd of 180 pedigree Simmentals and grows 300 acres of barley.

Scottish Green Party Ariane Burgess

Ariane Burgess is the Scottish Green Party’s lead candidate for the Highlands and Islands region. Ariane chose to make the Highlands her home when she returned to Scotland after living in the US for twenty years. She is dedicated to finding constructive ways in which we can respond to the two most urgent emergencies we’re facing today: climate and nature. She’s also well aware of the regional emergencies we’re facing: depopulation and the challenges to our mental health – heightened by the rapid changes to the ways we live and loss of work as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic.

She’s been involved in a wide range of sustainability projects from starting community gardens and orchards, project managing one of the region’s first cohousing projects, to teaching people sustainable village design. During the first lockdown she coordinated the grassroots outpouring in her community that saw neighbours assisting neighbours to get food and medicines.

Through her work she’s seen first-hand the incredible success communities have with locally-owned renewable energy, community investment, car share schemes and other community-led initiatives as well as the genuine impulse people have to work together for the common good.

It is her hope that her experiences of turning creative thinking and sustainable design into tangible benefits for communities can be an asset at Holyrood.
