Support in Mind, A Community Approach to Mental Health

Monday 25th January, 12.30 - 13.15

We hear a lot about mental health these days! What’s going on with it? Why does it matter? We hear that ‘Mental Health is Everyone’s Business’ – if that is the case then what can we all do about it? Join Ros Halley of the mental health charity Support in Mind Scotland for a workshop on developing A Community Approach to Mental Health.

Ros has many years’ experience of working in community development in Scotland’s regions. Since 2017, she has been working with the mental health charity and the National Rural Mental Health Forum to develop a new approach to supporting mental health. This ground-breaking work shows clear indications that this new way of thinking about tackling mental ill-health can galvanise people across the country to take collective action on the ground.

In this 45 min workshop, Ros will talk about what a Community-Led Mental Health approach means, why it could transform the way that we deliver mental health support and how we all can contribute to improving societal wellbeing. Ros will share her experience of community-based research in Scotland’s rural regions and highlight the challenges that exist and what could be done to help turn things around.

The workshop will be an opportunity to explore how everyone can build their own knowledge and adapt their own actions to make Scotland a better place for mental health.

Skills Development Scotland, Industry 4.0

Monday 25th January, 12.30 - 13.15

Technological and societal disruptions are coming at us thick and fast. Whilst we cannot predict the future, we can prepare for a future that is increasingly unpredictable.

Scotland’s citizens need the skills not only to cope with the change but to thrive in it, as well as to take advantage of, and create their own change.

SDS has been leading a programme of work to understand what these changes mean for the workplace, and the skills that will be required for this. This future focussed work is driving innovation in our products and services to make sure Scotland’s citizens and employers are prepared for this complex future.

This thought-provoking interactive workshop intends to stop and make you think how prepared are you for the future of work and what skills do you have that will support your lifelong career journey.

We will discuss:

  • Industry 4.0 & Scotland 2035
  • Meta Skills and their role in ensuring Scotland’s people and economy thrive
  • Future job trends

Ewan Stuart is a Careers Adviser with Skills Development Scotland working out of our Oban office. Ewan has a particular interest in the impact technology will have for the future of work.

RASASH, Talking About Consent

Join Romy Rehfeld from the Rape and Sexual Abuse Service Highland (RASASH) for a 'Talking About Consent' Workshop.

In this participatory workshop, we’re talking about consent - what it looks like, why it’s important, and what your rights are. We’ll be discussing issues such as alcohol and consent, consent in the digital world, and answering any questions you may have. Please note that in talking about consent and sex, we’ll also be touching on the topic of sexual violence, which may be triggering to some people.

RASASH provides confidential, non-judgemental practical and emotional support to survivors of sexual violence (age 13+) and their non-abusing partners, family, and friends. We operate a support line by phone, text, and email – details can be found at https://www.rasash.org.uk
